BJB Management Operating System (MOS)

A structured framework for running the firm with discipline, accountability, and alignment

v1.0 · March 2026 ⏳ Loading...

The Management Operating System (MOS) is BJB's structured framework for running the firm, inspired by EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System). It provides a complete set of tools and disciplines to align the leadership team, drive accountability, and ensure consistent execution across all departments. MOS is built on six key components:

🔭

Vision

Where are we going and why?

👥

People

Right people, right seats

📊

Data

Weekly scorecards drive accountability

⚡

Issues

Identify, Discuss, Solve

⚙️

Process

Document and follow the core processes

🎯

Traction

Rocks + meeting pulse = execution

The V/TO captures the firm's vision and plan on two pages. It answers eight fundamental questions:

1. Core Values

The guiding principles that define BJB's culture and decision-making

2. Core Focus (Purpose / Niche)

Why BJB exists and what it does best

3. 10-Year Target

The big, long-range goal that drives the firm forward

4. Marketing Strategy

Target market, three uniques, proven process, guarantee

5. 3-Year Picture

Revenue, profit, measurables, and what BJB looks like in 3 years

6. 1-Year Plan

Revenue/profit targets and 3-7 goals for the year

7. Quarterly Rocks

The 3-7 most important priorities for the next 90 days

8. Issues List

Strategic issues to be solved, parked, or eliminated

Note: The V/TO is a living 2-page document reviewed quarterly at the annual and quarterly planning sessions.

IDS Methodology

I — Identify

Name the root issue, not the symptom. Dig deeper with "what's the real issue here?"

D — Discuss

Open, honest, solutions-focused discussion. Everyone speaks. Stay on topic. No tangents.

S — Solve

Decide on the solution. Assign a To-Do (7-day action item) or Rock (90-day priority) with a single owner.

Note: IDS is the heartbeat of the Level 10 Meeting. Spend 60 of 90 minutes here.

Quarterly Planning (Full Day — 8 Steps)
  1. Review V/TO
  2. Review prior quarter Rocks
  3. Review Scorecard trends
  4. Set next quarter Rocks
  5. Identify strategic issues
  6. IDS the issues
  7. Set next quarter budget
  8. Cascading message
Annual Planning (2-Day Extended Session)

Extended session to reset the V/TO, establish 1-year plan, set annual goals, define Q1 Rocks, and align the leadership team.
