Error & Consistency Risk

With ~103 inputs scored across 6 phases, paralegals and attorneys experience score fatigue — leading to “3 across the board” entries. The biggest opportunity is in Case Opening (59 inputs) and Written Discovery (64 re-score inputs), where consolidation from sub-metrics to category-level scores preserves the actionable signal while dramatically reducing cognitive load.

12. Risk Mitigation

Addressing Every Concern Across All Phases

Concern: Collapsing 4 sub-metrics to 1 loses detail.

Mitigation: Each category score includes an optional free-text notes field (e.g., “Reachability is low primarily because of Response Time”). If any sub-metric ever proves independently actionable, it can be promoted back to a standalone field with zero formula impact.

Concern: Litify data may not accurately reflect the true score.

Mitigation: Every auto-derived field includes a manual override with a required reason field. 60-day shadow-mode validation runs both auto and manual scores side-by-side to measure agreement before going live.

Concern: Combining LSI+SLI and CSI+RDI may lose important distinctions.

Mitigation: Every single factor maps to the new system (see Section 11). The MCHS formula already averaged CSI+RDI into a single slot — we’re formalizing what the math already did. Factor weights within LCI and CCI preserve the relative importance of each original input.

Concern: Required Litify data sources may not exist or be reliable.

Mitigation: Worst case: 4 CPI auto-derived fields revert to manual, making the total ~24 manual instead of ~20. The CP consolidation (28→7), liability merge (9→5), and coverage merge (10→4) still deliver the majority of the benefit independently of CPI automation.

Concern: If Case Opening CP is consolidated but Treatment Monitoring CP refresh still uses the old 4 sub-metric structure, scores will be inconsistent across phases.

Mitigation: Treatment Monitoring CP refresh automatically inherits the consolidated structure. The refresh form will show the same 7 category-level scores, not the old 28 sub-metrics. The system enforces structural consistency across phases.

Concern: Attorneys may resist consolidating TSS from 8 to 5 factors, arguing each factor captures distinct negotiation dynamics.

Mitigation: TSS consolidation is marked as optional “Review Opportunity” — not a requirement. If attorneys prefer all 8 factors, total impact is only 3 extra inputs on a one-time scoring event. The recommendation stands as-is with no forced change.
