BJB Scoring Review — Management Recommendation

Reduce manual inputs from 59 to ~20 while preserving 100% of analytical insight

v1.0 • February 2026

1. Executive Summary

The Core Recommendation

Reduce the BJB scoring system from 59 manual 1–5 inputs + 6 checkboxes per case to ~20 manual inputs + ~12 auto-derived/formula fields while keeping the MCHS formula structurally identical. Every current insight is preserved — nothing is lost, only consolidated.

Error & Consistency Risk

With 28 CP sub-metrics scored at every stage progression, paralegals experience score fatigue — leading to “3 across the board” entries. When Contactability, Response Time, Follow-Through, and Appointment Reliability all get the same score, the granularity produces noise, not signal. The category-level score (e.g., “Reachability”) is what actually drives decisions.

8. MCHS Formula: Unchanged

Structurally Identical — Only Input Names Change

Current MCHS

(SLI × 0.25) + (TSI × 0.25) + (CP × 0.20) + ((CSI + RDI) / 2 × 0.15) + (CPI × 0.15)

Proposed MCHS

(LCI × 0.25) + (TSI × 0.25) + (CP × 0.20) + (CCI × 0.15) + (CPI × 0.15)

Key point: The formula weights and structure are identical. LCI replaces SLI at the same 0.25 weight. CCI replaces the (CSI+RDI)/2 average at 0.15 — the averaging was already happening, we just formalize it as a single composite. TSI and CP slots unchanged. CPI slot unchanged (auto-derived fields still produce a single CPI score).

10. Risk Mitigation

Addressing Every Concern

Concern: Collapsing 4 sub-metrics to 1 loses detail.

Mitigation: Each category score includes an optional free-text notes field (e.g., “Reachability is low primarily because of Response Time”). If any sub-metric ever proves independently actionable, it can be promoted back to a standalone field with zero formula impact.

Concern: Litify data may not accurately reflect the true score.

Mitigation: Every auto-derived field includes a manual override with a required reason field. 60-day shadow-mode validation runs both auto and manual scores side-by-side to measure agreement before going live.

Concern: Combining LSI+SLI and CSI+RDI may lose important distinctions.

Mitigation: Every single factor maps to the new system (see Section 9). The MCHS formula already averaged CSI+RDI into a single slot — we’re formalizing what the math already did. Factor weights within LCI and CCI preserve the relative importance of each original input.

Concern: Required Litify data sources may not exist or be reliable.

Mitigation: Worst case: 4 CPI auto-derived fields revert to manual, making the total ~24 manual instead of ~20. The CP consolidation (28→7), liability merge (9→5), and coverage merge (10→4) still deliver the majority of the benefit independently of CPI automation.

12. Next Steps

Action Plan

  1. 1

    Approve Direction

    Management sign-off on the consolidation approach outlined in this document.

  2. 2

    Verify Litify Data Sources (2-Day Audit)

    Confirm that billing records, task timestamps, document counts, and demand/offer data exist and are reliable in the BJB Litify org.

  3. 3

    Finalize Merged Weights (Attorney Workshop)

    Half-day session with lead attorneys to confirm LCI and CCI factor weights reflect their judgment priorities.

  4. 4

    Update Dev Spec

    Revise Litify Dev Summary and phase-specific specs to reflect the new consolidated scoring structure.

  5. 5

    Build Phase 1 with Shadow Mode

    Implement new scoring fields alongside existing ones. Both systems run in parallel — new scores auto-calculated, old scores still entered manually.

  6. 6

    Validate 60 Days

    Compare old vs. new scores across the portfolio. Measure agreement rate, identify edge cases, adjust weights if needed.

  7. 7

    Go Live

    Retire old scoring fields, switch to consolidated system. Old data preserved for historical reference.
