Part A — Process Phases (1–7)

Non-Party Depositions Through IME Management

Objective: Secure approval, prepare deposition notices, and coordinate court reporters for non-party witness depositions.
#TaskAssigned ToBadgeSLA
1Obtain Attorney Approval to Begin Non-Party Depositions
Approval GateAttorney must authorize commencement of non-party depositions. Confirm case posture supports taking depositions at this time.
ParaRequiredBefore any scheduling
2Prepare Deposition Notice & Cover Letter
Notice PreparationDraft deposition notice identifying witness, date, time, and location. Prepare cover letter for service. Ensure compliance with jurisdiction-specific notice requirements.
ParaRequired5 business days from approval
3Schedule Court Reporter
Court ReporterBook certified court reporter for the deposition. Confirm availability, location logistics, and real-time reporting if needed.
ParaRequiredConcurrent with notice prep
Exit Condition: Notice served, court reporter confirmed → Deposition proceeds on scheduled date.
Objective: Prepare and serve deposition notices/cross-notices for defendant witnesses, coordinate reporters and interpreters, and manage rescheduling.
#TaskAssigned ToBadgeSLA
1Prepare Deposition Notice / Cross-Notice
Notice / Cross-NoticeDraft deposition notice or cross-notice for defendant witnesses. Include all required statutory information. Cross-notices respond to defense-initiated depositions.
ParaRequired5 business days
2Schedule Court Reporter & Interpreter (if needed)
Reporter & InterpreterBook certified court reporter. If language interpreter required, coordinate certified interpreter availability. Confirm all logistics.
ParaRequiredConcurrent with notice
3Verify / Reschedule Deposition
VerificationConfirm deposition 5 business days prior. If rescheduling required, coordinate with all parties, update notice, and re-book reporter/interpreter.
ParaC / NA5 days before depo date
Exit Condition: Defendant deposition completed and transcript ordered.
