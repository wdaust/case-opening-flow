Part B — Post-Arbitration Scoring Pack

Attorney Scoring — Complete Within 24 Hours After Arbitration (5–8 minutes)

Purpose: How likely is this case to reach trial?

Rate each factor 0–20 (higher = more likely):

FactorScale
Defense rigidity0–20
Authority gap0–20
Liability dispute intensity0–20
Damages dispute intensity0–20
Venue/judge settlement pressure0–20
TPI = sum of all factors (0–100)
0–30: Likely to settle31–60: Uncertain61–100: High trial probability

Purpose: Provide a rational verdict forecast.

Low

High
$___
WEV = (0.25 × Low) + (0.50 × Most Likely) + (0.25 × High)

Purpose: How strong is the case at trial?

FactorScale
Liability confidence0–10
Plaintiff credibility0–10
Witness support0–10
Medical proof strength0–10
Expert strength0–10
Defense impeachment exposure (reverse)0–10
Theme clarity0–10
Venue fit0–10
TSS = average × 10 (0–100)

Purpose: Realistic economic value adjusted for trial strength.

ATV = WEV × (TSS / 100)

The number leadership uses for rational capital decisions.

Purpose: Did arbitration improve or weaken leverage?

FactorScale
Defense offer movement increased0–4
Neutral feedback favored plaintiff0–4
Defense revealed vulnerability0–4
Plaintiff presentation strengthened0–4
Defense signaled trial readiness (reverse)0–4
SLS = sum (0–20)
0–8: Leverage decreased9–14: Neutral15–20: Leverage increased

Purpose: Know what it costs to get to verdict.

  • Remaining expert costs
  • Depositions remaining
  • Trial prep hours
  • Expected trial days
  • Other hard costs
CTT = total projected cost-to-go

CTT Ratio

CTT Ratio = CTT / ATV
<10%: Strong economics10–20%: Watch>20%: Expensive risk

Purpose: Are we ready to try this case?

FactorScale
Discovery completeness0–4
Experts retained + ready0–4
Motions posture ready0–4
Witness prep plan defined0–4
Trial plan/outline formed0–4
TRS = sum (0–20)
0–8: Not ready9–14: Developing15–20: Ready

Purpose: Is trying this case economically smart?

FactorWeight
TPI40%
CTT Ratio score30%
Collectability certainty15%
Venue volatility15%
ERI = weighted sum (0–100)
0–40: Economically favorable41–70: Leadership decision71–100: High economic risk

Purpose: Attorney confidence in the forecast.

1Low confidence — major unknowns
2Some confidence — assumptions remain
3Moderate confidence — good visibility
4High confidence — facts mature
5Very high confidence — minimal unknowns
Required when 1–2: “What is the biggest unknown?”
