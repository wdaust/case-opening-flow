3 OA — 14-Point Orientation Call

Para / Legal Asst 24 biz hrs from assignment ⌄
Objective: Conduct the full Orientation Appointment covering all 14 agenda points. Gate: all OA Quality Gate fields must be populated before status can advance to OA Completed.

14-Point Agenda

1Introduce self and firm — who we are, what we do, how we win
What to coverState your name, role, and direct contact. Name the lead attorney. Explain the firm's specialization in personal injury. Give a brief plain-language explanation of how contingency fee works ("you pay nothing unless we recover"). Set a professional but human tone — this is often the first real conversation the client has with the firm.
2Explain the litigation process — timeline, stages, what to expect
What to coverWalk through the major stages: case opening → treatment → demand → negotiation → litigation if needed. Give realistic timelines (most cases: 12–24 months). Explain that delays often come from treatment completion and carrier behavior, not the firm. Emphasize that no news is often good news — the team is working in the background. Set expectation: client will hear from team at milestone events.
3Set communication expectations — preferred method, best time, language if applicable
What to coverAsk explicitly: best phone number, best time to call, preferred channel (phone/text/email). Ask about language preference — arrange interpreter if needed before next contact. Explain how the client portal works if available. Confirm: the client should NEVER speak to the other side's insurer or attorney without calling the firm first. Populate BJB__Preferred_Contact_Method__c and BJB__Preferred_Contact_Time__c before call ends.
4Review the incident — confirm facts, date, location, all parties, police report
What to coverConfirm: exact date, time, and location of incident. All vehicles/parties involved. Police report number and whether client has a copy. Any citations issued (to client or defendant). Weather, road, lighting conditions. Any emergency transport. Ask if client has given any recorded statements to anyone. Note any factual inconsistencies from intake notes for attorney review.
5Review injuries and current treatment status — providers, gaps, compliance
What to coverList all current body parts affected. All providers seen to date (ER, PCP, specialist, PT, chiropractor). Any imaging performed (MRI, X-ray, CT). Ask explicitly: any gaps in treatment? Any appointments missed? Is client still treating? If treatment has stopped, why? Flag any gaps >14 days for attorney review. Emphasize: consistent treatment is critical to case value.
6Identify all insurance coverage — client auto, health, PIP, med-pay, at-fault carrier
What to coverCollect: client's own auto carrier + policy number, health insurance carrier + member ID, PIP/no-fault status, med-pay availability, at-fault defendant's carrier (if known). Ask if client has UIM/UM coverage. Ask if client is on anyone else's policy (parent, spouse). Note: this feeds directly into the OA Burst letters and lien requests that fire immediately after call ends.
7Collect benefit flags — Medicare / Medicaid / ERISA / VA Benefits (Yes/No/Unknown for each)
What to coverAsk directly for each: Are you on Medicare? Medicaid? ERISA-governed health plan (employer-sponsored)? VA benefits? Record each as Yes / No / Unknown — never leave blank. "Unknown" triggers follow-up but doesn't block OA gate. A "Yes" on any of these triggers lien request tasks in the OA Burst. Medicare/Medicaid Yes = immediate lien protection obligations.
8Evidence inventory — photos, video, witnesses (Complete / Pending / None for each)
What to coverAsk client: Do you have photos of the scene / damage / injuries? (Complete / Pending / None). Any video footage (dash cam, surveillance, body cam)? Any witnesses who saw the incident? For each: get names and contact info if available. "Pending" means the client needs to look or gather — create follow-up task. "None" is acceptable — document it. OA Burst will fire evidence request letter automatically after call.
9Prior injuries disclosure — any prior claims, accidents, treatment to same areas (flag if Yes)
What to coverAsk directly: any prior accidents, injuries, or claims? Any treatment to the same body areas before this incident? Any prior lawsuits? If Yes to any: get dates, providers, case numbers if available. Set BJB__Prior_Injuries_Flag__c = Yes — this automatically creates an attorney notification task (BJB_Risk_Alert flow) due within 24 hrs. Prior injuries are not disqualifying but must be managed proactively.
10Defense contact flag — has client been contacted by other party's insurer or counsel? (flag if Yes)
What to coverAsk: Has anyone from the other party's insurance company called or written to you? Has any attorney reached out? Has anyone asked you to give a recorded statement? If Yes to any: get details (who, when, what was said, whether client gave any statement). Set BJB__Defense_Contact_Flag__c = Yes — immediate attorney notification via BJB_Risk_Alert flow. A recorded statement already given is a critical case-management event.
11Social media guidance — do not post, lock accounts, do not accept new followers
What to coverExplain clearly: defense investigators actively monitor social media. A single photo or post contradicting injury claims can destroy case value. Instructions: do not post about the accident, injuries, or legal case. Set all accounts to private. Do not accept new followers or friend requests from unknown people. Do not delete existing posts (spoliation risk). This guidance should be repeated at every subsequent client contact.
12DocuSign engagement letter + HIPAA authorizations — walk through, collect signature
What to coverSend DocuSign link before or during the call. Walk client through the engagement letter — explain contingency fee %, termination rights, scope of representation. Explain HIPAA authorizations and why they're needed (to obtain medical records). Do not end the call without confirming the client can access DocuSign. If client cannot sign during call, set a hard follow-up task for within 24 hours. BJB__Engagement_Letter_Signed__c = TRUE is required for the Final Gate.
13Collect all provider names for medical records requests — at least first treating provider required
What to coverCollect name, address, and phone for every provider: ER/hospital, PCP, specialists, PT, chiro, mental health, pain management. At minimum: the first treating provider after the incident is required to pass the OA gate. Set BJB__Provider_List_Initiated__c = TRUE once any provider is entered. OA Burst will fire records request letters to all entered providers immediately after call completion.
14Set next steps — what client should expect, timeline, how to reach the team
What to coverSummarize what happens next: records requests going out today, complaint being drafted, what the client will receive in writing. Give the paralegal's direct contact information. Tell client when they will hear from the team next (set expectation). Confirm the preferred contact method noted earlier. Remind: continue treatment, keep all appointments, contact us before speaking to anyone about the case. End on an affirming, confident note.
