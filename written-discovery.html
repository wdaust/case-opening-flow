2 Discovery Appointment — 11-Point Agenda

Attorney Required Within 5 days of connection ⌄
Objective: Attorney-led discovery appointment covering all 11 agenda points. Must be completed within 5 business days of client connection.

11-Point Agenda

1Opening & Expectations
Opening & ExpectationsSet expectations for the discovery process, explain what will be asked, and confirm client availability for depositions if needed.
2Accident Event Reconstruction
Accident Event ReconstructionWalk through the accident in chronological detail. Capture timeline, sequence of events, and any discrepancies from prior statements.
3Liability Risk Scan
Liability Risk ScanAssess any comparative fault, pre-existing conditions, or facts that may weaken liability. Document mitigation strategies.
4Injury & Treatment Timeline
Injury & Treatment TimelineMap all injuries to treatment providers, dates, and current status. Identify any gaps or inconsistencies.
5Prior History & Defense Discovery Preview
Prior History & Defense Discovery PreviewReview prior accidents, claims, lawsuits, medical history. Prepare client for defense discovery requests.
6Damages Deep Dive (Economic + Life Impact)
Damages Deep DiveCalculate economic damages (medical bills, lost wages, future care). Document non-economic impact on daily life.
7Insurance & Coverage Capture
Insurance & Coverage CaptureConfirm all applicable insurance policies: auto, UM/UIM, health, MedPay. Identify coverage limits and stacking.
8Evidence & Documentation Intake
Evidence & Documentation IntakeCollect photos, dashcam footage, witness info, police reports, medical records authorizations.
9Communication Norms & Client Responsibilities
Communication NormsSet communication expectations: response times, preferred contact method, document submission deadlines.
10Red Flags / Escalation Check
Red Flags / Escalation CheckScreen for credibility issues, social media risks, prior inconsistent statements, surveillance vulnerability.
11Confirm Next Steps & Assignments
Next Steps & AssignmentsSummarize action items for client and firm. Set follow-up dates. Confirm document submission deadlines.
Post-Appointment Note: After the discovery appointment, scores should be reassessed based on new information obtained. See Phase 3.

⚠ Exception Queues

⌄
Trigger Conditions: Cases enter exception queues when any of the following conditions are met.
  1. Draft overdue >30 days
  2. Client unreachable after 9 attempts
  3. Management escalation triggered
  4. Discovery appointment not scheduled within SLA
  5. Scoring not completed within 1 hour
  6. Finalization overdue >2 days
  7. Approval overdue >6 days
  8. Service overdue >10 days
  9. Good faith letter not sent at 60 days
  10. Aging file >90 days without activity

☆ Client Scoring

⌄
Purpose: Five scoring dimensions assessed during and after the discovery appointment to evaluate client risk and case readiness.

1. Reachability (1–5)

How easy is it to contact the client for discovery-related communications?

5 = Always reachable, responds same day 1 = Unreachable, requires escalation

2. Compliance Risk (1–5)

Likelihood of the client following instructions and meeting deadlines.

5 = Fully compliant, proactive 1 = Non-compliant, ignores deadlines

3. Defense Risk (1–5)

Vulnerability to defense discovery requests and cross-examination.

5 = Strong position, no vulnerabilities 1 = Highly vulnerable, major risks

4. Credibility Stability (1–5)

Consistency of client statements across all interactions and documents.

5 = Fully consistent, no discrepancies 1 = Major inconsistencies identified

5. Amendment Likelihood (1–5)

Probability of needing to amend discovery responses based on new information.

5 = Unlikely to need amendments 1 = Amendments almost certain
